# QQQ Geopolitical Dips Are Short-Lived

**Thesis:** QQQ tends to recover quickly from geopolitical-driven dips as tech sector resilience outweighs temporary fear, making bearish trades on geopolitical news risky unless exited rapidly.
**Applies to:** QQQ
**Tags:** geopolitical, mean-reversion, tech-resilience, puts, transition, dte-medium
**Confidence:** low
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-13

## Analysis

QQQ has demonstrated ability to bounce back from fear-driven dips within 24-48 hours. During the March 2026 Iran conflict scare, QQQ dropped ~1.3% on oil supply fears but recovered nearly all losses by the following morning. This suggests:

1. Tech sector fundamentals (earnings, growth narratives) often override short-term geopolitical fears
2. Money rotation out of tech during crisis fears tends to reverse quickly
3. Buying puts on QQQ during geopolitical fear spikes requires same-day or next-morning exit discipline

The pattern appears tied to QQQ's status as a liquidity favorite — when systemic fears ease, capital flows back into large-cap tech rapidly.

## Exceptions & Nuances

- Prolonged conflicts with actual economic impact (not just headline fears) may break this pattern
- Fed policy divergence during geopolitical stress could override tech resilience
- QQQ below key SMAs (50-day at $613.89, 200-day at $590.65) during entry suggests mixed technical setup — may affect recovery speed
