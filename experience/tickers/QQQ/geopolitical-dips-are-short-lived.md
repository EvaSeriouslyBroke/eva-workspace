**Thesis:** QQQ tends to recover quickly (within 3–7 trading days) from geopolitical-driven dips, but mean reversion call plays are only viable if the stock remains positioned above its 200-day SMA; breaks below the 200 SMA invalidate this recovery pattern.

## Analysis

The QQQ "geopolitical fear fade" pattern relies on the large-cap tech sector's balance sheet resilience. When price action is driven by macro headlines rather than tech fundamentals, a mean reversion recovery is a high-probability trade.

However, Hindsight analysis from across March 2026 has solidified two non-negotiable boundaries:
1. **The 200 SMA is the Floor:** Mean reversion call theses are invalidated instantly if the 200-day SMA breaks. Temporary geopolitical "dips" become structural regime shifts once this level is lost.
2. **Trend Persistence:** A technical breakdown (e.g., price below 50 SMA) remains active even if the news catalyst (e.g., oil price spike) fades. Structural trend is higher priority than news status.

**Key Hindsight Insight (March 20, 2026):**
The QQQ $590C and $595C trades confirm that **speed of exit is the primary alpha** when a technical floor breaks. By exiting within 4 hours of entry at losses of -1.5% and -8% as the 200 SMA failed, Eva saved significant capital. Holding through the daily close would have resulted in losses twice as large.
