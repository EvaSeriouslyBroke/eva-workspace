# Geopolitical Fear Trades Fade Quickly

**Thesis:** Oil and geopolitical-driven market fears often dissipate faster than expected, making directional trades based on these catalysts risky unless the move is captured immediately.
**Applies to:** general
**Tags:** geopolitical, oil-volatility, fear-trades, momentum, transition, dte-medium
**Confidence:** low
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-13

## Analysis

Geopolitical shocks (Middle East conflicts, oil supply disruptions) create immediate market reactions that can be profitable for quick momentum plays. However, the "fear premium" embedded in options during these events decays rapidly when:
- The feared outcome doesn't materialize immediately
- Markets adapt to the new information
- Underlying macro trends reassert themselves

The challenge is distinguishing between sustained geopolitical-driven moves and temporary fear spikes. Entry timing is critical — buying into elevated IV on fear means fighting time decay while waiting for the thesis to play out. When the fear subsides quickly, even correct directional theses can result in losses due to IV crush and theta decay.

## Exceptions & Nuances

- Sustained conflicts with actual supply disruptions (not just fears) may create longer-lasting moves
- Energy sector (XLE) may behave differently than tech (QQQ) during oil shocks
- Entry on day 1 of fear spike vs day 2+ makes significant difference
